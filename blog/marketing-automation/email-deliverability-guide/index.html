Email Deliverability Best Practices: A Complete Guide

You can craft the perfect email, but it's worthless if it lands in spam. Here's everything you need to know about email deliverability—from authentication to list hygiene.

What is Email Deliverability?

Email deliverability measures your ability to reach subscribers' inboxes. It's not the same as delivery rate (whether an email was accepted by the server)—deliverability specifically measures inbox placement versus spam folder.

The Three Pillars of Deliverability

1. Authentication (Technical Foundation)

Email authentication proves you are who you say you are. Without it, mailbox providers can't distinguish your legitimate emails from phishing attempts using your domain.

SPF (Sender Policy Framework)

SPF tells receiving servers which IP addresses are authorized to send email on behalf of your domain. Add a TXT record to your DNS that lists your email service providers.

DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail)

DKIM adds a digital signature to your emails. The receiving server verifies this signature against a public key in your DNS, proving the message wasn't altered in transit.

DMARC (Domain-based Message Authentication)

DMARC tells receiving servers what to do when SPF or DKIM fail. It also enables reporting, so you can see who's sending email using your domain.

Google & Yahoo 2024 Requirements

As of February 2024, Google and Yahoo require bulk senders (5,000+ emails/day) to have SPF, DKIM, and DMARC configured. Without them, your emails may be blocked entirely.

2. Reputation (Trust Score)

Your sender reputation is like a credit score for email. Mailbox providers track your sending patterns and user engagement to decide if you're trustworthy.

Reputation is built on:

  • IP Reputation — The sending IP address's history
  • Domain Reputation — Your domain's sending history
  • Content Reputation — Patterns in your email content
  • Engagement Signals — Opens, clicks, replies, complaints

3. Content (What You Send)

Even with perfect authentication and reputation, bad content triggers spam filters:

  • Excessive use of spam trigger words ("FREE!", "Act now!", "Limited time!")
  • ALL CAPS in subject lines or body
  • Too many images with little text
  • Broken HTML or rendering issues
  • Missing unsubscribe link
  • Misleading subject lines

List Hygiene: Your Most Important Practice

The quality of your email list directly impacts deliverability. Sending to invalid or unengaged addresses signals to mailbox providers that you're not a legitimate sender.

  • Remove hard bounces immediately (invalid addresses)
  • Suppress soft bounces after 3-5 consecutive failures
  • Implement double opt-in for new subscribers
  • Remove subscribers who haven't engaged in 6-12 months
  • Never purchase email lists (this will destroy your reputation)
  • Use email validation services before importing lists
  • Honor unsubscribe requests within 24 hours
Never Buy Email Lists

Purchased lists contain spam traps, invalid addresses, and people who never consented. One send to a bad list can tank your reputation for months.

Warming Up a New IP or Domain

New IP addresses and domains have no reputation. Sending high volume immediately looks suspicious. Instead, gradually increase volume over 4-6 weeks.

A typical warmup schedule:

  • Week 1: 50-100 emails/day to most engaged subscribers
  • Week 2: 500-1,000 emails/day
  • Week 3: 5,000-10,000 emails/day
  • Week 4: 25,000-50,000 emails/day
  • Week 5+: Gradually reach full volume

Monitoring Your Deliverability

Track these metrics to catch problems early:

  • Inbox Placement Rate — Percentage reaching inbox (use tools like GlockApps or 250ok)
  • Bounce Rate — Should be under 2%
  • Spam Complaint Rate — Should be under 0.1%
  • Unsubscribe Rate — High rates signal content problems
  • DMARC Reports — Shows authentication failures and potential abuse

Platform-Specific Considerations

Gmail

Gmail's algorithms heavily weight user engagement. If recipients don't open or click your emails, you'll gradually move to the Promotions tab or spam. Focus on subject line optimization and send-time personalization.

Microsoft (Outlook, Hotmail)

Microsoft is stricter about authentication and reputation. Use their SNDS (Smart Network Data Services) to monitor your reputation with their network.

Yahoo/AOL

Yahoo aggressively filters based on engagement signals. Maintain clean lists and segment by engagement level to protect your reputation.

Quick Wins to Improve Deliverability

  1. Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC if you haven't already
  2. Remove subscribers who haven't opened in 6+ months
  3. Add a clear unsubscribe link to every email
  4. Use a consistent "From" name and address
  5. Test emails across providers before sending
  6. Segment your list by engagement level
  7. Send consistently—don't go from 0 to 100,000 overnight
