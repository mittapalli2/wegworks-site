AEM 6.5 End of Life: LTS vs Cloud — Which Path Should You Choose?

With AEM 6.5 heading towards End of Life (core support ends Feb 2027, extended support Feb 2028), organizations face a critical decision: stick with Long-Term Support or take the leap to AEM Cloud? Let's dig into the real questions and help you find the smartest path forward.

Timeline Alert: AEM 6.5 core support ends February 2027. Extended support continues until February 2028. If you haven't started planning your migration, now is the time.

The AEM 6.5 End of Life Timeline

Before we dive into the LTS vs Cloud debate, let's be crystal clear about what "End of Life" means for AEM 6.5 and the critical dates you need to know:

Now – December 2025
Full support with regular service packs, security patches, and feature updates. This is your planning and preparation window.
February 2027
Core Support Ends. No more regular service packs or feature updates. Only critical security patches provided.
February 2028
Extended Support Ends. No more patches of any kind. You're on your own. Security vulnerabilities will go unpatched.

Why Upgrade? The Real Benefits

Is upgrading just about staying supported, or are there real benefits to moving forward? The answer is: both.

Security & Compliance

Running unsupported software isn't just a technical risk—it's a business and legal one. Many compliance frameworks (PCI-DSS, HIPAA, SOC 2) require running supported, patched software. After February 2028, you may find yourself out of compliance simply by running AEM 6.5.

Performance & Scalability

AEM Cloud Service offers automatic scaling, global CDN integration, and performance optimizations that simply aren't possible on legacy infrastructure. Organizations migrating to Cloud typically see:

  • 40-60% improvement in page load times due to integrated CDN
  • Zero-downtime deployments with blue-green deployment patterns
  • Automatic scaling during traffic spikes (think Black Friday or product launches)
  • Reduced operational overhead — Adobe manages infrastructure

Innovation Velocity

AEM Cloud receives continuous updates with new features every few weeks. Staying on 6.5 means missing out on:

  • Adobe Sensei AI/ML capabilities for auto-tagging and smart crops
  • Enhanced Edge Delivery Services for lightning-fast sites
  • Improved Content Fragment and headless capabilities
  • Native integrations with Adobe Experience Platform

LTS vs. Cloud: The Honest Comparison

There's no one-size-fits-all answer. Both paths have legitimate use cases. Here's an honest comparison:

Factor AEM LTS (On-Premise/AMS) AEM Cloud Service
Updates Manual service packs (quarterly) Automatic, continuous updates
Infrastructure You manage (or Adobe Managed Services) Adobe manages everything
Customization Unlimited (OSGi bundles, JCR mods) More constrained (immutable deployments)
Deployment Traditional CI/CD, manual Cloud Manager, Git-based, automated
Cost Model License + infrastructure costs Consumption-based pricing
Migration Effort Lower (upgrade in place) Higher (code refactoring required)
Long-term Support Eventual EOL (plan for next migration) Evergreen (always current)

What's Working for LTS?

LTS Advantages

  • Stability: Predictable release cycles, no surprise changes
  • Deep customization: OSGi bundles, workflow modifications, JCR operations
  • Familiar infrastructure: Your ops team knows how to manage it
  • Regulatory control: Some industries require on-premise data residency
  • Lower immediate cost: Avoid migration project investment

What's Tempting About Cloud?

Cloud Advantages

  • Zero operational overhead: Adobe handles patching, scaling, monitoring
  • Always current: Automatic updates mean you're never "behind"
  • Better developer experience: Modern CI/CD with Cloud Manager
  • Performance: Global CDN, auto-scaling, optimized architecture
  • Future-proof: Adobe's primary investment focus is Cloud

Pain Points & Unexpected Wins

Cloud Migration Pain Points:

  • Custom OSGi bundles need refactoring for Sling Content Distribution
  • Direct JCR/CRX access patterns must change
  • Some legacy integrations break and need rebuilding
  • Learning curve for Cloud Manager and Git-based deployments
  • Content migration can be complex for large repositories

Unexpected Wins from Cloud Migration:

  • Development velocity often increases after initial adjustment
  • Ops teams freed up for higher-value work
  • Incident response improves (Adobe's SRE team is watching)
  • Performance gains often exceed expectations
  • New features "just appear" without upgrade projects

Who Should Go Where?

Based on our experience migrating dozens of AEM implementations, here's our honest guidance:

Consider LTS If You...

  • Have heavy customizations — Deep OSGi modifications, custom workflows, JCR-level integrations
  • Face regulatory constraints — Healthcare, financial services, government with strict data residency requirements
  • Need more time — Can't execute a migration before 2027 and need a bridge strategy
  • Have limited budget — Need to spread migration costs over multiple years
  • Run mission-critical workloads — Zero tolerance for any change risk in the short term

Consider Cloud If You...

  • Want to reduce ops burden — Infrastructure management consumes too much time/money
  • Need global scale — Serving audiences across multiple geographies
  • Value innovation — Want access to new features as they ship
  • Have modern code — Already using best practices, minimal legacy debt
  • Plan for the future — Want to migrate once and be done (no more EOL cycles)
Hybrid Approach: Some organizations choose a phased approach—moving to a newer LTS version now, then migrating to Cloud later. This spreads risk and cost, but also means two migrations instead of one.

End-State Business Value: Measuring Success

What does success look like after the upgrade or migration? Here's how to measure the impact on your business:

Technical Metrics

  • Page load time: Track Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS) before and after
  • Deployment frequency: How often can you ship changes?
  • Incident rate: Number and severity of production issues
  • Time to recovery: MTTR for incidents
  • Availability: Uptime percentage

Business Metrics

  • Content velocity: Time from content creation to publication
  • Conversion rates: Impact of performance improvements on conversions
  • Operational costs: Infrastructure, support, and maintenance spend
  • Time to market: Speed of launching new digital experiences
  • Team satisfaction: Developer and author experience ratings

What Success Looks Like

Cloud Migration Success Story: One of our retail clients saw a 52% improvement in page load times, 3x increase in deployment frequency, and 40% reduction in operational costs within 6 months of completing their Cloud migration. Their Black Friday traffic spike was handled automatically without any manual intervention.

Making Your Decision

Here's a simple framework for making this decision:

Step 1: Assess Your Current State

  • What version of AEM 6.5 are you on? (Latest service pack?)
  • How many custom OSGi bundles do you have?
  • What integrations exist? (Legacy vs. modern APIs)
  • How large is your content repository?
  • What's your current infrastructure setup?

Step 2: Define Your Constraints

  • Budget: What can you invest in migration?
  • Timeline: When must you be off AEM 6.5?
  • Team capacity: Do you have internal expertise or need partners?
  • Risk tolerance: How much change can the business absorb?

Step 3: Evaluate Both Paths

Run a technical assessment for both LTS upgrade and Cloud migration. Understand the effort, risk, and timeline for each. Don't assume one is better—validate with data.

Step 4: Align with Business Strategy

Where is your organization heading? If digital transformation and agility are priorities, Cloud aligns better. If stability and cost control are paramount, LTS may be the right bridge.

Our Recommendation

If you can make the investment, AEM Cloud Service is the future. Adobe's primary R&D investment is in Cloud. New features ship there first. The "evergreen" model means you'll never face another EOL migration.

However, we recognize that not every organization can migrate immediately. If you need more time:

  1. Upgrade to the latest AEM 6.5 service pack to maximize your support window
  2. Start Cloud readiness assessment now — understand your migration scope
  3. Begin code modernization — refactor toward Cloud-compatible patterns even if staying on 6.5
  4. Plan your migration timeline — don't wait until 2027 to start
